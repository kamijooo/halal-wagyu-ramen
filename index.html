Our story

From real wagyu, a real bowl.

Our family started as wagyu merchants in 1962. Five generations of facing cattle, judging meat, and refining the same craft — wholesale, butcher, restaurant.

In my thirties, I visited America, and the "WAGYU" I saw there shocked me. Australian and American cattle being sold as wagyu, people smiling, saying "so this is wagyu?" — and what they ate wasn't the real thing.

As the fifth generation, I made a decision: deliver real wagyu to the world again. Not the over-fattened industrial cuts. Healthy lean wagyu with deep umami in the meat itself. That's the wagyu in this bowl.

And Kyoto — the heart of Japan's food culture — is where we serve it as ramen. Walk out of Kyoto Station, walk into this bowl. Halal-friendly, made for travelers who came to Japan for the real thing.

"Real wagyu, for everyone in the world." — 五代目 · Godaime Wagyu Tokyo Owner
